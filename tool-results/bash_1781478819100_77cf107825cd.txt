The world of AI training, data annotation, and RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human
Feedback) operates under heavy client confidentiality. Because these platforms primarily train
foundational models for major tech giants (like Google, OpenAI, Meta, Apple, and Microsoft),
they use internal project codenames to keep the specific model identities secret.
The major AI training platforms and a comprehensive breakdown of active, high-frequency, and
legacy projects running within their ecosystems include:
1. Outlier AI (Operated by Scale AI)
Outlier (which absorbed Remotasks) is currently the largest and most widely discussed platform.
It specializes heavily in domain-expert review, multi-turn logical evaluation, and high-tier
coding tasks.
Project Bulba (and Bulba Extensions): One of the most massive, longest-running active project
series. It focuses heavily on step-by-step reasoning, mathematical proof validation, and code
evaluation using structured formatting (often requiring markdown or LaTeX).
Project Aether: Active multi-turn dialogue project. Contributors focus on analyzing deep,
conversational logic, tracking context across 4 to 10 back-and-forth turns, and ensuring the AI
follows complex style instructions.
Project Flamingo / Flamingo Multimodal: A highly active branch dedicated to training
multimodal models. This involves analyzing combinations of text inputs alongside images,
temporal video files, and audio sync files.
Project Guitar: Focuses heavily on strict instruction-following, heavy constraints (e.g., "Write
this without using the letter 'e'"), and rewriting prompts to test model limits.
--- Page 23 ---
Project Dolphin: Broadly focused on search query refinement, web-grounding, fact-verification,
and technical summarization tasks.
Project T-Rex: A strict "fact-checking and anti-hallucination" project where trainers must use
external search tools to comprehensively audit every line of an AI's output.
Project Oneroi: High-tier, specialized reasoning tasks typically reserved for verified subject-
matter experts (STEM, Law, and Advanced Finance).
